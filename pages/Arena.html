Diary Entries

3rd of Heartfire, Turdas. 3E 289

I woke up in the Imperial Sewers, cold sweat clinging to me like a curse. A vision roused me—her name was Ria Silmane, a sorceress, or so she claimed. She was once the apprentice of Jagar Tharn, now slain in body but still tethered to this world by some unholy resolve. She told me I am her last hope. The Emperor, she said, is imprisoned in a plane of Oblivion, while Tharn wears his face like a mask and rules in his stead.

It took me two days to claw my way out of those stinking tunnels—goblins in the shadows, giant rats gnawing at everything that lives or stinks of life. Who tends these places? No one. I scavenged what I could, dodged death more than once, and walked out with a few trinkets worth selling.

Now I sit in Shornhelm, a frigid city in High Rock. Strange as it sounds, I miss the sewer’s staleness—the stillness there. Here, the cold gnaws at my joints. I’ve rented a single room at the King’s Bird, an inn just crooked enough to remind me of home. Thirty septims bought me three nights. May they be enough.

Ah—and I almost forgot the greater madness. Silmane spoke of my “Imperial Duty.” The Staff of Chaos. An unbreakable artifact forged by the Land itself, now shattered and scattered across Tamriel. Each fragment sealed, hidden. One lies in a place called Fang Lair, somewhere in the Dragon’s Teeth Mountains—at least, that’s what the ghost-witch whispered before fading from my vision. I’ll need to ask around. Maybe High Rock remembers.

In the meantime, the innkeeper here, Hargabyth the Warrior (I don’t see it), gave me a simple errand—deliver a gold bar to the Elite Equipment Store for 32 gold. He could’ve paid me in ale and rent, but no, I suppose coin is coin. And I need it.
